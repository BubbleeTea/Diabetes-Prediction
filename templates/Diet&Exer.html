Diet

Morning

Breakfast
  • Opt for whole-grain cereals like oatmeal or whole-grain bread.
  • Include proteins such as eggs, low-fat yogurt, or tofu.
  • Add fruits like berries, which are lower in sugar compared to other fruits.
  • Avoid sugary cereals and opt for unsweetened alternatives.

    • Afternoon

    Lunch
  • Focus on lean proteins like grilled chicken, fish, or legumes.
  • Include plenty of non-starchy vegetables like broccoli, spinach, and cauliflower.
  • Choose complex carbohydrates like brown rice, quinoa, or whole wheat pasta in moderation.
  • Limit high glycemic index foods like white rice and refined grains.

    • Evening

    Dinner
  • Similar to lunch, focus on lean proteins, non-starchy vegetables, and moderate portions of whole grains.
  • Grilled or roasted vegetables are a good side dish.
  • Avoid fried foods and heavy sauces, as they can be high in unhealthy fats and sugars.

    • Night

    Before Bed
  • A small, balanced snack can help stabilize blood sugar levels overnight.
  • A piece of whole-grain toast with a thin spread of nut butter or a small handful of nuts can be a good choice.
  • Avoid heavy or large meals close to bedtime.
